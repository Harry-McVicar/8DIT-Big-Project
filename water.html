Hydropower

Hydropower follows the concepts of energy transformation as the gravitational potential energy of the water is converted into kenetic energy and finally into electrical energy when they pass through turbines along their way down the damn. This is a similar process to wind power as the kenetic energy of the water molecules must be used to optimise rotatios of the water turbines and hence the amount of electrical energy produced. The electrical energy can be stored and used as electricity both domestically and commercially as required. This is similar to tidal power in which turbines are placed at on the ocean floor and the predictable movement of the water with the tide causes the turbines to spin, thus producing electricity.
